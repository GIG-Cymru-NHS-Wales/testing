Reason for Admission

Mrs. Jones was admitted via A&E with a 3-day history of increasing shortness of breath, chest pain, and productive cough with green sputum. She had a background of mild asthma.

Clinical Findings

On examination, patient was pyrexial (38.5°C), tachypnoeic (RR 24), with reduced air entry and coarse crepitations in the right lower zone. Chest X-ray confirmed right lower lobe pneumonia.

Treatment Given

Patient was treated with intravenous antibiotics (Co-amoxiclav 1.2g TDS for 5 days), nebulized bronchodilators, and supportive care including oxygen therapy. She responded well to treatment with resolution of fever and improvement in respiratory symptoms.
